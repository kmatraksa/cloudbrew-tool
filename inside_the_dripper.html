04Pour Technique · Choose by goal

Pour technique = agitation control = compound class selector. You choose the technique based on what you want to extract, not based on what "tastes good." Click a technique to see its water flow path and extraction pattern.

05Dripper Anatomy · Internal mechanics

A dripper is not just a container — it is a flow controller that determines how water passes through the coffee bed. Cone geometry, hole size and position, ridge depth, and how the filter attaches to the wall — all of these create different flow patterns that directly affect extraction uniformity and the final flavor in your cup.

06Molecular Theatre · The molecules inside

From the "compound classes" in the graph, let's meet the real molecules in your coffee cup. Real structures, real reactions, real interactions with your tongue. Click any molecule for an in-depth look.

Method

Compound curves are heuristic representations based on first-order extraction kinetics and Arrhenius scaling. Actual extraction varies with bean, grind, water profile, and technique.

Note

Built as a coaching tool. Use alongside brew logging and your own sensory observations to refine your understanding.
